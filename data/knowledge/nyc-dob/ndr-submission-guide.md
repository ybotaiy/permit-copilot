# Next Day Review (NDR) Submission Guide

## NDR Eligibility

Next Day Review is limited to the following:

- Minor Alterations for interior Residential spaces
- Commercial Storefronts
- Facade Repair
- Limited Scope (Mechanical, Plumbing, Structural, and subsequent applications)

**No Alt-CO or Enlargements shall be filed for Next Day Review.**

By electing Next Day Review, applicant confirms there is no change in egress, use or bulk that may require a new/amended Certificate of Occupancy.

## Title Block Standards

All submitted drawings must have a Title Block with:

- **DOB Building Plan Job Number** — lower right side of each sheet
- **Architect/Engineer of Record information** — lower right side of title block
- **Drawing reference number** — for PAAs, a revision decimal (e.g., A-100.05)
- **Page numbers** — lower right side (e.g., PG – 3 of 45)
- **Drawing sheet name or description** — identifying floor, elevation, section
- **Project name and address** — top right side of title block
- **Revision block** — indicating dates of previous submissions
- **Signed and sealed Stamp** by the Architect or Engineer (drawings without stamp will be rejected)
- **Space/box for DOB Plan Examiners** to place Approval stamps

Standard Lettering size should be 1/8" minimum.

Sheets marked "not for construction" will not be accepted.

## Drawing Requirements

- Compliance with DOB Graphic Standards
- Scale of each drawing or detail
- North arrow orientation must match on all plans and details
- Dimensions related to scope and area of work
- Walls and partitions at appropriate width for scale (single line not acceptable)
- Fire rated walls and doors must be labeled with a tag
- All habitable spaces must show daylight and ventilation calculations
- Details must be clear, legible, and specific to the proposed construction project
- All unrelated and unnecessary details must be deleted from templates
- Notes and schedules must be relevant and properly referenced

## Energy Code Compliance

2020 Energy Conservation Construction Code of New York State (2020 ECCNYS), based on the 2018 edition of the International Energy Conservation Code and ASHRAE 90.1-2016, became effective on May 12, 2020.

The 2020 New York City Energy Conservation Code (2020 NYCECC), based on the 2020 ECCCNYS, aligns with certain provisions of the NYSERDA NYStretch Energy Code-2020 (as required by Local Law 32 of 2018).

Types of Energy Analysis:
- Tabular
- REScheck
- COMcheck
- Energy Modeling

Energy Conservation Note (when applicable): "To the best of my knowledge, belief and professional judgment, these plans and specifications are in compliance with the NYCECC – NYC Conservation Construction Code 2020, using Chapter ____ [specify chapter]."

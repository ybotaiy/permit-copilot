# Jersey City Zoning Review and Determination Overview

The Jersey City Zoning page explains when applicants need a Zoning Determination Letter (ZDL), when they need a Zoning Review Application (ZRA), which checklists apply to different project types, and how zoning review connects to construction permitting.

## Do I Need a ZDL or ZRA?

A Zoning Determination Letter (ZDL) is a letter provided in response to a request regarding the classification of uses and the interpretation and applicability of Jersey City's Zoning and Land Use Ordinances and Redevelopment Plans.

A Zoning Review Application (ZRA) is the first step for anyone in the process of new construction, rehabilitation, tenant fit-out, solar panel installation, or other work that requires a building permit or approval from the Historic Preservation Office.

## Zoning Review Application Checklists

The page states that Zoning Review Application Checklists are available and should be reviewed before submission so the application contains the information necessary to apply successfully.

The listed checklist types are:

- New Construction
- Addition
- Exterior Renovations
- Interior Renovations
- Exterior/Interior Renovations
- Tenant Fit-Out
- Minor Work
- Historic
- Signage
- Certificate of Occupancy
- Land Use Board Approved Plans

## Zoning Approval Workflow Updates

The page notes that, as of November 15, 2024, each full set of construction documents receives a red "Released" stamp in the upper right-hand corner with details of the specific application upon approval of a Zoning Review Application.

The zoning signature set is uploaded to the online portal and is required to be submitted as the construction document set in the Construction Permit Application.

## How to Update a ZRA Approval

To update a ZRA approval, applicants are directed to contact the review planner assigned to the application. The review planner unlocks the application so new documents can be uploaded, and the applicant must submit complete drawing sets with changes from the previous approval bubbled.

## Zoning Determination Letters

The page repeats that a ZDL may address whether a specific use is permitted in a zoning district or whether a use may retain legal nonconforming status.

For short-term rentals, the Division of Zoning oversees the permitting process and enforcement of the ordinance. Applicants for a short-term-rental permit must first obtain a Zoning Determination Letter - Short Term Rental to include in the permit application.

## Zoning Review Applications

The page states that a ZRA is a prerequisite for work requiring permits and verifies that the proposed plan complies with Jersey City's Land Use and Zoning Ordinances and Forestry Standards.

It also notes that, as of April 1, 2024, the Division of Zoning makes determinations on whether an application is "exempt" or "not exempt" from the Residential Development Fee (RDF).

## Zoning Review Applications - Signage

The page states that a ZRA - Signage is the first step for anyone installing signage on a property, and all signage requires zoning approval before a building permit application is submitted to the Division of Construction Code.

## Certificate of Occupancy Review

Applications for Certificate of Occupancy (CO), Temporary Certificate of Occupancy (TCO), and Continued Certificate of Occupancy (CCO) are reviewed by the Division of Zoning for compliance with Jersey City's Land Use and Zoning Ordinances and Forestry Standards.

The page also notes that a CO is not required for the buying, selling, or transfer of property if a CO already exists for the current use.
